Orthogonality of vectors

Two vector X,Y are said to be orthogonal on each other when their dot product result to zero.

X⃗⋅Y⃗=0\vec{X} \cdot \vec{Y} = \mathbf{0} X⋅Y=0

Y⃗⋅X⃗=0\vec{Y} \cdot \vec{X} = \mathbf{0} Y⋅X=0

OR\mathbf{OR} OR

∥X∥2+∥Y∥2=∥X+Y∥2\| \mathbf{X} \|^2 + \| \mathbf{Y} \|^2 = \| \mathbf{X} + \mathbf{Y} \|^2 ∥X∥2+∥Y∥2=∥X+Y∥2

The Same Dot product can be represent in terms of Linear algebra notation

X⃗⋅Y⃗≡X⊤Y=0\vec{X} \cdot \vec{Y} \equiv \mathbf{X}^\top \mathbf{Y} = \mathbf{0} X⋅Y≡X⊤Y=0

Y⃗⋅X⃗≡Y⊤X=0\vec{Y} \cdot \vec{X} \equiv \mathbf{Y}^\top \mathbf{X} = \mathbf{0} Y⋅X≡Y⊤X=0

From above defination we can say that Zero Vector is perpendicular with every vector.

0⃗⋅X⃗=0\mathbf {\vec{0}} \cdot \vec{X} = \mathbf{0} 0⋅X=0
